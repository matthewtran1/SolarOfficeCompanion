Our Vision

The need for this project is to reduce the use of power grids by using photovoltaic cells

Photovoltaic cells have gotten increasingly cheaper and have become viable to use as a renewable energy source

Our project, the Solar Office Companion (SOC), is designed as a window/wall mounted solar powered DC power source for powering small electronic devices

By increasing the use of photovoltaic cells, we can:

Reduce the use of power grids provided by hydro Reduce the amount of damage to the environment Slowly transition into renewable energy sources

Our team's ultimate goal is to provide an innovate solution to help combat climate change, transition to clean energy and promote health and safety
The team aims to design a relatively small stand-alone photovoltaic system for consumer use with the following requirements:

Provide clean and renewable energy for personal home/office use Supply low voltage DC to power consumer electronics Use photovoltaic (PV) cells to provide clean energy

Design

The system is designed to only cover a small portion of a window or wall and is currently designed for use with DC electronics only
This design is aimed at office workers and home integration
Our prototype design is a modular system that mainly consists of a solar panel manager, solar modules and a battery bank

Solar Power Manager

Solar Power Manager

  • The solar panel module used is the DFR0535 board
  • The board is mounted with pcb standoffs inside of a plexiglass enclosure
  • This module is used in solar street lights and environmental sensing modules
  • This component was chosen for its low price point while still meeting our design requirements
  • This module can extract more energy from the sun with a range of outputs when the output is not close to the
    maximum power voltage

    • Features:

  • Solar Input Voltage Vpmin = 7V - 30V
  • Maximum Battery Charge Current IpmBAT = 2A
  • Battery Bank Voltage VpmBAT = 3.7V
  • Output Current IpmO = 1.5A
  • Solar Charge Efficiency η1A = 78%

    • Solar Module

  • The solar module used is the POW921360 module
  • These cells were chosen as they are small yet able to provide enough power to meet design requirements
  • The modules for the prototype are made up of two panels wired in series and placed in folded sheet metal mounts
  • The panels must be in series to meet the minimum 7V input voltage requirement for the solar power manager

    • Features:

  • Output Voltage VpvO = 5.5V
  • Current IpvO = 0.54A
  • Output Power PpvO = 3W w/ Efficiency η = 16%
  • Area = 0.02208 (LxW = 0.160m x 0.138m)

    Battery Module

    Battery Module

  • The battery cells used is the rechargable 18650 batteries
  • The input voltage of the battery cells match the solar power manager battery input voltage
  • They are versatile and don't require extra hardware to connect to the solar power manager
  • They are inexpensive and easily available
  • They can be combined in parallel and a minimum of 4 cells are needed to provide sufficient charging capability

    • Features:

  • Nominal Voltage VBAT = 3.7V
  • Charge Capacity = 2600 mAh
  • Energy capacity of a single cell is obtained as followed:
    Ah * VBAT = EBAT
    EBAT = 2.6 Ah * 3.7 V = 9.62 Wh
  • Two configurations of 4 and 8 cells can be made as seen on the left.


    • Other components include:

  • Load: The load will be a cellphone (Google Pixel 3a) as it should be the minimum an energy system can power
  • Casing/Enclosures: Plexiglass is used given its low cost and relative workability
  • Mounting/Placement: The module folded 3/32" aluminum sheet metal backing allows the module to be
    placed on a window
